# Neutron Stars

Neutron stars are extremely dense stellar remnants formed after the core-collapse supernova of a massive star.

## Key properties

- Typical mass: about 1.1 to 2.3 solar masses
- Typical radius: about 10 to 14 km
- Composition: primarily neutrons, with possible exotic matter in the core
- Magnetic fields: can be enormously strong
- Rotation: can spin rapidly; pulsars are rotating neutron stars observed via periodic emission

## Why they matter in gravity

Neutron stars are important laboratories for strong gravity, dense matter, and relativistic astrophysics. Binary neutron star mergers also produce gravitational waves and heavy elements.
